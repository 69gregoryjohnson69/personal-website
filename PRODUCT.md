# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Gregory Johnson Jr. is a **humanities scholar first** — a PhD student in Spanish at the University of Iowa, a bilingual (English/Spanish) educator, and an emerging public- and digital-humanities practitioner. **People are the focus of his work**: students, communities, and the cultures that live inside a language. He is *not* a software engineer, and the site must never position him as one; any digital-humanities work is in service of people and ideas, not a tech career.

Primary visitors:
- **Faculty, graduate committees, and fellow scholars** assessing whether he's a serious, thoughtful researcher and teacher.
- **Hiring and selection committees** for teaching posts, fellowships, and public-humanities or community-facing roles.
- **Collaborators and community partners** in education, libraries, and cross-cultural work.

Their context: they're deciding, fairly quickly, whether this is a person worth their time and trust. The job to be done is simple — *"Who is he, what does he study and teach, and is he someone I'd want to work with or learn from?"*

## Product Purpose

A personal academic portfolio that presents Gregory as a scholar-educator whose work connects literature, culture, language, and people. It should:

1. **Show real projects** — course design, public-humanities and advocacy research, and the scholarly communities he helps build. The flagship is his Spanish writing course, *Fundaciones de la Escritura en Español: Edición futbolera*.
2. **Convey his teaching** — what and how he teaches, and the humane pedagogy behind it.
3. **Give a clear, human picture of who he is** — bilingual, community-minded, rigorous but welcoming.

Success: a visitor leaves with a *specific* sense of him as a humanities scholar and teacher, has seen genuine intellectual work, and has an easy path to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Earnest, warm, and sincere — a scholar-teacher who believes, openly and without irony, in human connection and the transforming power of language. People and students lead; the tone is hopeful and idealistic, never glib, never "tech," never clever for its own sake.

- **Voice:** first-person and declarative — "I believe," "I want," "I hope." Considered, layered sentences. Mirrors his own teaching-philosophy prose (challenge and comfort working symbiotically, human connection, "my years abroad transformed my worldview"). Match this register in all copy. Do **not** write dry wit, ironic asides, or clever-for-its-own-sake turns.
- **Three words:** earnest, warm, humane.
- **Emotional goal:** the visitor feels they've met a generous, sincere teacher-scholar who genuinely cares about people, and would trust him with their students or their project.

## Anti-references

- **A software-developer / "tech-bro" portfolio.** He does not build software for a living; do not frame him as an engineer. Digital humanities is a tool for humanistic inquiry, not a career pivot.
- **A cold CV-as-webpage** — a bulleted list of credentials with no human in it.
- **Generic SaaS / startup template** and **corporate sterility** — gradient hero blobs, metric cards, lifeless committee tone.
- **Over-decorated academic cliché** — stock ivy-and-library photography, brass-plaque pomposity.

## Design Principles

1. **People at the center.** Every section should foreground people — students, communities, cultures, and Gregory himself — over abstraction.
2. **Make the rigorous approachable.** Mirror his teaching: present serious scholarship in a way that invites rather than intimidates.
3. **Show the work in his words.** Let his real writing and projects carry the credibility; preserve his voice and only correct glaring errors.
4. **Bilingual and cultural by nature.** Spanish and cross-cultural life are part of the identity, not decoration — let them show (with correct typography and `lang` markup).
5. **Craft as respect.** Careful typography and restraint signal respect for the reader's time, the same respect he extends to students.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, visible keyboard focus, full keyboard navigation, semantic HTML with landmarks, meaningful alt text.
- Mark Spanish-language passages with `lang="es"` so screen readers pronounce them correctly; render accents and `¿ ¡ ñ` properly.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`: every animation has a reduced or instant alternative.
- Readable by default: generous line-height, a 65–75ch measure for prose, real text (never baked into images).
